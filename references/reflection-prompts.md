# Reflection Prompts Library

## Purpose

This is a collection of prompts organized by situation. Use them when the user
seems stuck, unclear, or wants to explore something more deeply but doesn't
know where to start.

**Important:** These are not interview questions to ask in sequence. They are
tools to choose from based on what the moment needs. One well-chosen prompt
beats ten generic ones.

---

## How to Choose a Prompt

**Match the prompt to the situation:**
- What are they trying to understand? (pattern, feeling, decision, etc.)
- What's their emotional state? (overwhelmed, stuck, curious, etc.)
- What's the level of specificity needed? (broad exploration vs concrete focus)

**Introduce prompts with framing:**
- "I'm curious —" followed by the question
- "What if we looked at it this way:" followed by the angle
- "You don't have to answer this, but I'm wondering —" followed by the prompt

**If a prompt doesn't land:**
- That's useful data. Try a different angle.
- Or pause and reflect: "That question didn't seem to fit. Let's try a different approach."

---

## Starting Fresh — "I Want to Reflect, But Don't Know Where to Start"

**Broad entry points:**
- "What's been on your mind lately, even if it doesn't seem important?"
- "What's something you've been avoiding thinking about?"
- "What's the emotion you've felt most this week?"
- "If you had to describe your current state in three words, what would they be?"
- "What's something you wish you understood about yourself?"

**For complete stuckness:**
- "What feels heaviest right now?"
- "What's the easiest thing to talk about?"
- "What would you tell a friend if they asked how you're doing?"

---

## Emotional Overwhelm — "Everything Feels Like Too Much"

**Grounding in the present:**
- "What's one thing that's actually okay right now?"
- "What's true in this room, in this moment?"
- "What's one thing your body is telling you right now?"
- "If you could only deal with one thing today, what would it be?"

**Creating some distance:**
- "If this was happening to someone else, what would you notice?"
- "What would you say to a friend in this situation?"
- "If you were watching this in a movie, what would you think?"
- "What about this will still matter in a week? In a month?"

**Breaking it down:**
- "What's the most urgent part of this?"
- "What's one tiny thing you actually have control over?"
- "What's one thing that could wait until tomorrow?"

---

## Feeling Stuck — "I Keep Doing the Same Thing"

**Exploring the pattern:**
- "What happens right before you do [the thing]?"
- "What does [the thing] give you in the moment?"
- "What do you tell yourself right after you do it?"
- "What's the upside of staying in this pattern?"

**Understanding the resistance:**
- "What would change if this pattern broke?"
- "What feels scary about doing it differently?"
- "What are you afraid might happen if you stopped?"

**Finding the leverage point:**
- "What's one thing that's already different, even a little?"
- "What's the smallest version of change you can imagine?"
- "What's one thing that makes it harder than it needs to be?"

---

## Decision Fatigue — "I Can't Decide"

**Clarity on values:**
- "What matters most to you in this?"
- "What kind of person do you want to be in this situation?"
- "What would you respect yourself for doing here?"
- "What would future you be glad you did?"

**Testing the options:**
- "If you chose option A, how would you feel a week from now?"
- "What's the advice you'd give someone else in this situation?"
- "What's the part of you that wants option A? What about option B?"
- "What happens if you don't decide?"

**Finding permission:**
- "What would you choose if no one else's opinion mattered?"
- "What would you choose if you couldn't fail?"
- "What's the choice you're afraid to name?"

---

## Self-Criticism — "I'm Too Hard on Myself"

**Developing self-compassion:**
- "What would you say to a friend who was in this situation?"
- "What's something you handled recently that you might be undervaluing?"
- "What's a time you were harder on yourself than the situation deserved?"
- "What would change if you spoke to yourself the way you'd speak to someone you care about?"

**Naming the inner critic:**
- "What does the voice that criticizes you sound like?"
- "Where did you learn to be this hard on yourself?"
- "What is that voice trying to protect you from?"
- "What would happen if that voice was a little quieter?"

**Finding a middle ground:**
- "What's one thing you actually did well, even if it wasn't perfect?"
- "What's something you're tolerating that you don't have to?"
- "What would 'good enough' look like here?"

---

## Relationship Struggles — "Something's Off Between Us"

**Clarifying your own experience:**
- "What do you wish they understood about you?"
- "What's something you're not saying that needs to be said?"
- "What do you need in this situation that you're not getting?"
- "What's your part in this, if you're honest?"

**Understanding the dynamic:**
- "What happens to you when you're around them?"
- "What patterns show up when the two of you interact?"
- "What's true about them that you're not letting yourself see?"
- "What's true about you that you're not letting yourself see?"

**Finding your boundaries:**
- "What are you accepting that you don't want to accept?"
- "What would you say if you weren't afraid of their reaction?"
- "What's one thing you need for your own wellbeing in this?"
- "What would staying look like if it felt healthy? What about leaving?"

---

## "Why Am I Like This?" — Self-Understanding

**Exploring origins:**
- "Where did you learn to be this way?"
- "What in your history might have shaped this?"
- "What environments has this trait been useful in?"
- "What's the upside of being this way, even if it also has downsides?"

**Reframing traits:**
- "What's another way to describe this quality that sounds less like a problem?"
- "In what contexts is this actually a strength?"
- "What would this trait look like if it was in balance?"
- "What would you say to this part of you if it was a friend?"

**Acceptance vs. change:**
- "What's the part of this that's actually okay about you?"
- "What's the part you genuinely want to change?"
- "What would accepting this part of yourself change?"

---

## "I Don't Know What I Feel" — Emotional Literacy

**Body-based prompts:**
- "Where do you feel that in your body?"
- "What sensation is happening right now?"
- "Is it more tight, heavy, restless, or something else?"
- "What would this feeling say if it could speak?"

**Emotional menu:**
- "Would it fit better to say you feel angry, sad, scared, or something else?"
- "What's the mix? Is it more frustration, or disappointment, or hurt?"
- "What's the emotion beneath the emotion?"
- "What are you not letting yourself feel?"

**Naming without diagnosing:**
- "What would you call this feeling if it didn't have a label?"
- "What's the quality of this feeling — sharp, dull, heavy, light?"
- "If this feeling was a weather pattern, what would it be?"

---

## Procrastination — "I Keep Putting It Off"

**Understanding the block:**
- "What happens in your mind when you think about starting?"
- "What do you imagine will happen once you begin?"
- "What's the worst part, really?"
- "What are you afraid might happen if you finish?"

**Finding the leverage:**
- "What's the smallest possible version of this task?"
- "What if the goal was just to open the document — not to do anything else?"
- "What if you did it badly on purpose, knowing you could fix it later?"
- "What's one thing that would make this 10% easier?"

**Reframing the resistance:**
- "What does procrastinating protect you from?"
- "What would change if this task were already done?"
- "What's actually true about your ability to do this?"

---

## "I Feel Like a Failure" — Identity and Worth

**Separating worth from achievement:**
- "What would still be true about you if this had gone differently?"
- "What do you value that isn't about performance or achievement?"
- "Who are you outside of what you produce or accomplish?"
- "What would someone who loves you say about how you're doing?"

**Contextualizing the experience:**
- "What's the part of this that's actually about something else?"
- "What's this situation bringing up from the past?"
- "What's true about this moment that you're not letting yourself see?"

**Finding the self beyond the moment:**
- "What's something you've handled that you're proud of?"
- "What's a time you didn't give up, even when it was hard?"
- "What do you wish you believed about yourself?"

---

## Life Transitions — "Everything Is Changing"

**Naming the loss:**
- "What are you grieving in this change, even if it's also positive?"
- "What's ending that you wish wasn't?"
- "What will you miss about how things used to be?"

**Finding the opportunity:**
- "What's becoming possible that wasn't before?"
- "What part of you is ready to emerge?"
- "What's something you're curious about in this new chapter?"

**Grounding in agency:**
- "What's still within your control, even in this uncertainty?"
- "What's one way you can show up for yourself right now?"
- "What's a tiny choice you can make today that feels like yours?"

---

## "I'm Burned Out" — Energy and Capacity

**Understanding the drain:**
- "What's been asking more than you have to give?"
- "What are you carrying that isn't yours to carry?"
- "What's been taking from you without giving back?"

**Finding restoration:**
- "What actually restores you, even a little?"
- "What's something you used to enjoy that you've stopped making time for?"
- "What would rest look like if it wasn't earned?"

**Rebuilding capacity:**
- "What's one thing you could say no to this week?"
- "What's one demand you could remove, even temporarily?"
- "What's the smallest version of showing up that still counts?"

---

## Anxiety and Worry — "I Can't Stop Thinking About It"

**Externalizing the worry:**
- "If this worry was a separate voice, what would it be trying to protect you from?"
- "What would happen if you let yourself believe the worst-case scenario?"
- "What's the probability of what you're worried about actually happening?"

**Finding the actual next step:**
- "What's one thing that's actually in your control?"
- "What's the tiniest action you could take right now?"
- "What would it mean to handle this imperfectly but adequately?"

**Creating a container:**
- "What if you gave yourself 10 minutes to worry about this, then moved on?"
- "What's one thing you could write down and stop carrying in your head?"
- "What's the difference between worrying and planning?"

---

## "I Feel Disconnected" — Loneliness and Belonging

**Understanding the disconnection:**
- "What kind of connection are you hungry for?"
- "What's true about your current relationships that isn't meeting that need?"
- "What are you not letting yourself need?"

**Finding the path back:**
- "What's one person you could reach out to, even just briefly?"
- "What's something you could share that would feel real?"
- "What's one way you could show up in a relationship that matters to you?"

**Self-connection:**
- "What's something you could do for yourself today?"
- "What's one way you could be on your own side?"
- "What would feel like coming home to yourself?"

---

## End-of-Day Processing — "Today Was..."

**For good days:**
- "What went well today that you might be overlooking?"
- "What's something you handled that you can feel okay about?"
- "What's one thing you're grateful for, however small?"

**For hard days:**
- "What was the hardest moment?"
- "What did you need today that you didn't get?"
- "What's one thing you can let yourself off the hook for?"

**For neutral days:**
- "What's one thing that happened?"
- "What's something you noticed today?"
- "What's one thing your body told you?"

## Using Prompts Effectively

**Do:**
- Choose prompts based on the specific situation
- Introduce prompts with gentle framing ("I'm curious —", "What if...")
- Honor their response, whatever it is
- Pause and reflect their answer before moving deeper
- Adapt or rephrase if the prompt doesn't land

**Don't:**
- Ask prompts in sequence like an interview
- Push past resistance (that's data, not a problem to solve)
- Use prompts to "get" them to a specific insight
- Rush the silence after asking (the processing happens there)
- Frame prompts as homework or assignments

The goal is not to get through all the prompts. The goal is to find the one
that opens something real.
