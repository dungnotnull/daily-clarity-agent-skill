# Pattern Library — Recognizing Life Patterns in Conversation

## How to Use This Guide

When a user shares something and you sense a pattern, check this library.
Always present patterns as possibilities, never as diagnoses.
Frame: "I wonder if..." / "I notice..." / "It sounds like..."

---

## TIME-BASED PATTERNS

### Sunday Anxiety / Sunday Scaries
**Signals:** "I always feel dread on Sunday evenings", "Sunday ruins my weekend",
"I can't relax on Sundays", feeling anxious without clear reason at week's end.

**What's usually happening:** Anticipatory anxiety about the upcoming week.
Often amplified if the work week feels out of control or if weekends feel
like the only "real" time.

**Gentle reflection:** "I wonder if Sundays are hard because they're when
the week ahead feels most real — and if the week feels unpredictable or heavy,
that can show up as dread even before anything has happened."

**Micro-habit direction:** Sunday wind-down ritual; brief look at Monday so it
feels less unknown; separating "preparation" from "worry".

---

### Monday Energy vs Friday Crash
**Signals:** Alternating high motivation at week start, complete depletion by end.

**What's usually happening:** Adrenaline-driven productivity early week,
running on empty by Thursday/Friday. May indicate over-scheduling or
poor energy management.

**Gentle reflection:** "It sounds like you're running at a high capacity
early in the week and the tank gets empty by Thursday. Bodies don't do well
when they're constantly sprinting — I wonder if some of that Friday crash
is the bill coming due for Monday's overdrive."

---

### Night Owl Guilt
**Signals:** "I do my best work at night but I feel guilty about it",
"I can never wake up early", "I'm a night person but everyone tells me I should change".

**What's usually happening:** Chronotype mismatch with social/work expectations.
May be genuine late chronotype, or avoidance behavior (night feels safer, more control).

**Gentle reflection:** "It sounds like your energy naturally shows up later
in the day, and there's a tension between that and what you feel like you
'should' be doing. Those don't always have to match."

---

### Post-Holiday / Post-Vacation Crash
**Signals:** Feeling more depleted after a break than before, can't get back into rhythm.

**What's usually happening:** The break revealed how exhausted they actually were.
The "crash" is the real baseline emerging. May also indicate the pre-break pace
is unsustainable.

---

### The 3pm Wall
**Signals:** Consistent afternoon energy dip, coffee dependence in afternoon,
feeling useless after lunch.

**What's usually happening:** Normal circadian dip (2-4pm). Often worsened by
blood sugar swings, poor sleep, or holding physical tension all morning.

## ENERGY PATTERNS

### The Empty Well
**Signals:** "I'm always tired", "I don't know why I'm so exhausted",
"I sleep but it doesn't help", "I feel like I'm running on empty".

**What's usually happening:** One or more of: chronic sleep debt, emotional labor
accumulation, poor recovery habits, always giving but rarely receiving, saying yes
too often, or untreated health issue (flag gently: "This level of tiredness is
worth mentioning to a doctor if it's been consistent").

**Gentle reflection:** "When you say you're running on empty — is it more
that you don't have the energy to start things, or that you start them but
don't have the energy to finish? Those can have different causes."

---

### The Social Battery Drain
**Signals:** "I love people but I'm exhausted after social events",
"I need days to recover after parties", "I feel guilty for not wanting to go out".

**What's usually happening:** Introversion or high sensitivity — neither is a problem.
The issue is often the guilt, not the need for recovery.

**Gentle reflection:** "What you're describing sounds like someone whose
social battery is real and finite. Needing time to recharge isn't a flaw —
it's just how some people are wired. The question is whether you're building
in enough recovery time, or whether you're pushing past what you actually need."

---

### The Boom-Bust Energy Cycle
**Signals:** Very high energy periods followed by crashes, doing too much then
being unable to do anything, inconsistent output.

**What's usually happening:** May be natural energy variation, or hypomanic/depressive
cycling (don't name this — observe and reflect). Often also appears in people with
ADHD-adjacent patterns: interest-based attention, hyperfocus then shutdown.

**Reflection approach:** Map the timeline — how long are the up periods? How long
the down? What triggers the shift? Don't diagnose; help them observe their own cycle.

---

### Emotional Eating / Comfort Behavior Patterns
**Signals:** Eating, shopping, scrolling, or drinking at specific emotional states.

**What's usually happening:** Regulation behavior — using external things to manage
internal states. Not a moral failure. Often signals an unmet need.

**Gentle reflection:** "It sounds like [behavior] shows up most when [state].
I wonder what that state is asking for — sometimes those behaviors are just
the brain's way of reaching for relief. The question is whether there's
something that could offer that relief that also feels better afterward."

## RELATIONAL PATTERNS

### The People Pleaser Spiral
**Signals:** "I said yes but I didn't want to", "I feel guilty saying no",
"I'm always the one who adjusts", "I don't want to disappoint them".

**What's usually happening:** Approval-seeking behavior rooted in fear of rejection
or conflict. Often exhausting. May be tied to specific relationships or universal.

**Gentle reflection:** "I notice you mentioned worrying about how [person] would
feel if you said no. I wonder — when you imagine saying no, what's the feeling
you're most trying to avoid?"

**Micro-habit direction:** The 24-hour rule ("I'll check my calendar and get
back to you") — creates space between request and response.

---

### The Over-Explainer
**Signals:** "I always feel like I need to justify myself", "I explain myself
too much", "I give long reasons for things I don't want to do".

**What's usually happening:** Fear that "no" alone isn't enough. Learned behavior
from environments where reasons were required. Can be exhaust both user and others.

---

### The Invisible Resentment Build
**Signals:** "I've been fine with everything but lately I just feel irritable",
"I feel resentful but I don't know why", "everything is fine but I'm angry".

**What's usually happening:** Resentment from accumulated unexpressed needs.
The resentment is a signal, not the problem.

**Gentle reflection:** "Resentment usually shows up when we've been saying
'it's fine' about things that aren't entirely fine. I wonder what you've been
giving up or adjusting to that you haven't named yet — even to yourself."

---

### Specific Person Energy Drain
**Signals:** Feeling exhausted or anxious after interactions with a specific person.

**What's usually happening:** The relationship has a pattern the user may not have
consciously named. May be the person is demanding, critical, or unpredictable.
May also be that the user suppresses themselves around this person.

**Reflection approach:** "What's different about how you feel during the interaction
versus after? And is there anything you find yourself not saying when you're with them?"

---

### The Comparison Spiral
**Signals:** Social media mentions, "everyone else seems to have it together",
"I feel behind", looking at others' lives and feeling inadequate.

**What's usually happening:** Comparing internal experience to others' external
presentation. Almost always unfair and inaccurate comparison.

**Gentle reflection:** "When you compare yourself to [them], are you comparing
to what you actually know about their life, or to what their life looks like
from the outside? Those are usually very different things."

---

## COGNITIVE PATTERNS

### The Overthinking Loop
**Signals:** "I can't turn my brain off", "I keep replaying conversations",
"I went over it a hundred times", "I can't stop thinking about it".

**What's usually happening:** Rumination — brain trying to solve an unsolvable
or uncertain situation through repetition. Thinking more doesn't help because
the issue isn't informational.

**Gentle reflection:** "It sounds like your mind is trying to think its way
to certainty about something that might not have a certain answer. What would
it mean if you didn't know the answer to this?"

**Micro-habit direction:** The "worry window" — scheduled 15 minutes to worry,
then deliberately redirect. Physical interruption (walk, cold water) to break loop.

---

### All-or-Nothing Thinking
**Signals:** "I always", "I never", "if I can't do it perfectly I won't start",
"I blew the diet so I might as well give up", "it has to be this way or not at all".

**What's usually happening:** Perfectionism or cognitive distortion.
The binary creates failure by design — because nothing is 100%.

**Gentle reflection:** "I noticed you said 'I always' — I'm curious if that's
literally always, or if it feels that way because the exceptions don't register
as much as the times it happens."

---

### Future Catastrophizing
**Signals:** "I'm worried that if X happens then Y will happen and then Z",
"the worst case scenario is...", elaborate worry chains.

**What's usually happening:** Anxiety trying to prepare for all possible futures.
Feels like planning but is actually amplifying fear.

**Reflection:** "Walk me through the very next step — just the immediate next
thing, not the whole chain. What would actually have to happen first for the
thing you're worried about to start unfolding?"

---

### The Impostor Cycle
**Signals:** "I don't know why they picked me", "I'm waiting for them to
figure out I'm a fraud", "I got lucky", dismissing their own accomplishments.

**What's usually happening:** Achievement doesn't update internal self-image.
Common in people who are actually competent (impostors usually aren't worried
about being impostors).

**Gentle reflection:** "I notice that when you talk about [achievement], you
describe it as luck or the other person's generosity — not as something that
happened because of what you brought. What do you think would have to be true
for you to take some of the credit for that?"

## BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS

### Procrastination Types (Very Different)

**Type 1 — Avoidance Procrastination:**
Avoiding something unpleasant. Signs: knows exactly what needs doing, avoids anyway.
Direction: make it smaller, make starting easier, understand the specific dread.

**Type 2 — Perfectionism Procrastination:**
Won't start because it has to be done right. Signs: lots of planning, researching,
never executing. Direction: constraint ("done is better than perfect"), permission
to produce a bad first draft.

**Type 3 — Overwhelm Procrastination:**
Can't start because too much. Signs: staring at the task list, paralyzed.
Direction: the ONE thing — pick the single most important task and ignore the rest.

**Type 4 — Interest-Based Procrastination (often ADHD-adjacent):**
Can only do things that are novel, urgent, or interesting. Signs: does last-minute
very well, long history of "not living up to potential". Direction: create urgency
or novelty artificially; body double; temptation bundling.

---

### The Self-Sabotage Pattern
**Signals:** "I always blow it when things are going well", "I got close and then
messed it up", "I don't know why I do this to myself".

**What's usually happening:** Safety in familiar failure; fear of success and
what comes with it; unconscious belief that good things don't last.

**Gentle reflection:** "I wonder what feels dangerous about things going well.
Sometimes we get close to something good and the unfamiliarity of it feels
more threatening than the comfort of the familiar struggle."

---

### The All-In / All-Out Pattern
**Signals:** "I go hard for two weeks then stop completely", "I'm either
obsessed with something or not doing it at all", inconsistent follow-through.

**What's usually happening:** Interest-based motivation (no middle gear),
or pendulum from restriction to release. All-or-nothing thinking applied
to behavior.

**Direction:** Design for "minimum viable" version — the version you can do
even on the worst day. The floor, not the ceiling.
