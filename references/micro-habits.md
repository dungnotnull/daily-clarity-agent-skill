# Micro-Habits — Design Principles & Library

## Core Philosophy

The goal is never the habit itself. The goal is the user feeling like
"I can actually do this" — which builds the identity of someone who follows through.

A habit suggestion that feels exciting but gets abandoned in 3 days is worse
than no suggestion at all — it adds to the pile of "things I failed at."

---

## The 6 Design Principles

### 1. Tiny Beats Big
The habit should feel almost embarrassingly small. If the user laughs and says
"that's it?" — that's the right size.

```
❌ "Start meditating for 20 minutes every morning"
✅ "What if you just sat for one minute before checking your phone?"
```

### 2. Attach to Existing (Habit Stacking)
New habits stick better when anchored to something already happening reliably.

```
Anchor examples:
- "After I pour my morning coffee..."
- "While my toast is in the toaster..."
- "Right after I brush my teeth..."
- "When I sit down at my desk..."
- "While the kettle boils..."
```

### 3. Design for the Worst Day
If a habit only works when motivation is high, it will fail. Design the version
that works even on the day everything goes wrong.

```
❌ "Go to the gym for an hour"
✅ "Put on your shoes and step outside — that's the whole habit.
    If you walk further, great. If not, you still did it."
```

### 4. Remove Friction
Every extra step is a chance to not do the thing. Reduce steps to near zero.

```
Examples:
- Keep the book on the pillow (read before sleep)
- Pre-fill water bottle the night before
- Put workout clothes next to the bed
- Keep the journal open to a blank page already
```

### 5. Experiment Frame, Not Commitment
"Forever" is paralyzing. "Let's see what happens" is approachable.

```
❌ "Commit to journaling every day"
✅ "Want to try this for just 3 days and see what you notice?"
```

### 6. Identity Over Outcome
Connect the action to who they're becoming, not just what they'll achieve.

```
❌ "Track your spending to save $200/month"
✅ "What if, for one week, you just noticed — without judgment —
    when you're about to buy something? Just notice. That's it."

(This builds the identity of "someone who's aware of their spending" —
the behavior change tends to follow naturally.)
```

## Micro-Habit Library by Goal Area

### Sleep
- "What if you put your phone across the room, just for tonight?"
- "What if the only rule was: lights off 10 minutes earlier than usual?"
- "What if you tried writing down tomorrow's first task before bed —
  just so your brain doesn't have to hold onto it overnight?"

### Movement
- "What if the habit was just putting on your shoes? Nothing else required."
- "What if you did one minute of stretching when you wake up — before
  you even get out of bed?"
- "What if every time you're on a phone call, you stood up or walked around?"

### Mental Clarity / Overthinking
- "What if you set a timer for 10 minutes to think about it as much as you
  want — and when the timer goes off, you write down the one thing still
  unresolved and let the rest go for now?"
- "What if you wrote down three things at the end of the day — doesn't
  matter what, just whatever's there?"
- "What if, when your mind starts looping, you said out loud: 'noted' —
  just to acknowledge it without engaging?"

### Connection / Relationships
- "What if you sent one text to someone you've been meaning to reach —
  just a one-line 'thinking of you'?"
- "What if, in your next conversation with [person], you tried asking
  one more question before responding with your own thought?"

### Boundaries / People-Pleasing
- "What if your new default response to requests was 'let me check
  and get back to you' — just to create a little space?"
- "What if you tried saying 'I can't this time' without adding a
  reason — just once, to see how it feels?"

### Energy / Burnout
- "What if you picked ONE thing on your list today and let everything
  else be optional?"
- "What if you took a 5-minute break BEFORE you feel like you need one —
  on a timer, not waiting until you're depleted?"
- "What if 'rest' for today just meant doing one less thing than yesterday?"

### Procrastination
- "What if the goal today was just opening the document — not writing
  anything, just opening it?"
- "What if you set a timer for 5 minutes and told yourself you could
  stop after that if you wanted?"
- "What if you did the task badly on purpose — just to get a rough
  version down, knowing you'll fix it later?"

### Self-Compassion
- "What if, the next time you're hard on yourself, you asked: 'would I
  say this to a friend in this situation?'"
- "What if you kept a running note of things you handled well — even
  small things — just for a week?"

## How to Offer a Micro-Habit

### Step 1: Ask Permission
"I have a small idea — want to hear it?"

### Step 2: Frame as Experiment
"This isn't about committing forever — just trying it for a few days
to see what happens."

### Step 3: Make It Concrete
Specific trigger + specific tiny action. No ambiguity.

### Step 4: Lower the Bar Further If Needed
If they hesitate: "Or even smaller — what if it was just [smaller version]?"

### Step 5: Offer Follow-Up (Optional)
"Want me to check in with you on this next time we talk? Totally fine if not."

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't suggest habits that require buying anything
- Don't suggest apps, trackers, or systems (defeats the "no system" promise)
- Don't stack multiple habits at once
- Don't frame failure to do the habit as a problem — "didn't happen" is data, not failure
- Don't follow up in a way that feels like checking homework — keep it light:
  "How did that little experiment go, if you tried it?"

---

## Reframing "Failure" to Try Again

If the user reports they didn't do the micro-habit:

```
✅ "That's useful information actually — what got in the way?"
✅ "No worries at all. Sometimes the size still isn't quite right —
    want to try an even smaller version, or a different idea altogether?"
✅ "That's really common, by the way. What do you think the real
    barrier was — time, energy, or just forgetting?"
```

Never:
```
❌ "That's okay, just try harder this time"
❌ "Consistency is key — let's try again"
❌ Anything that implies they failed at a test
```
